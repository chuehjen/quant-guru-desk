# Beth Kindig / I/O Fund — Analytical Framework

## The two-engine model

A faithful "Kindig" decision = **fundamental thesis (Beth Kindig) + technical/risk overlay (Knox Ridley)**. Ignoring the risk-management half produces a caricature.

- **Kindig** — fundamental research, thesis generation, stock selection, forward revenue/earnings modeling.
- **Ridley** — real-time execution, technical analysis, position sizing, hedging, raising cash.

The I/O Fund describes itself as **hedge-fund-like**: "actively manages risk through hedging and raising cash."

## Core principles

1. **Own the leader of a secular trend, early.** Want the dominant platform/standard in a multi-year structural shift, not the cheapest also-ran. Archetype: NVIDIA entry at $3.15 (split-adjusted) in Dec 2018.
2. **Thesis built on a durable moat.** Original NVIDIA thesis = the CUDA software moat ("developers self-regulate the number of competitors due to a need for a universal platform"). Look for winner-take-most dynamics.
3. **Forward revenue/earnings modeling.** Price off where revenue and margins are *going*, not trailing numbers.
4. **Margin expansion + operating leverage is the value-creation engine.** "Margin expansion," "GAAP profitability breakthrough," operating leverage.
5. **Bottleneck thinking.** Map a trend to its current binding constraint and invest there (compute → memory → power → software). "Time to power is the variable that matters most."
6. **Investor-vs-enthusiast opportunity-cost test.** "An investor must always answer — is my capital better deployed elsewhere?" This is why she trims even structural winners.
7. **Depth as edge.** "Dozens of hours per equity" of institutional-grade due diligence.

## The forward-model method (worked template)

The reproducible valuation pipeline:

1. **Decompose the fundamental drivers.** e.g., NVIDIA 70% upside = ~30% order/demand gap × ~40% higher Blackwell ASP × unit growth.
2. **Build the forward revenue line.** e.g., ~$50B/quarter data-center revenue; $210B Blackwell vs ~$130B Hopper.
3. **Apply gross-margin trajectory and operating leverage** to derive forward EPS / FCF.
4. **Convert to a forward multiple** that is *growth-adjusted* (she'll call a stock's valuation "eerily low" relative to forward earnings while it compounds).
5. **Cross-check against the opportunity set** — only hold if forward upside beats the rest of the book.

## Stock-selection signals (the screen)

- **Revenue growth & acceleration** — prizes *accelerating* growth, not just high growth.
- **Gross margin level & trajectory** — expanding margins central.
- **Operating leverage / path to GAAP profitability** — profitability breakthrough = major re-rating catalyst.
- **TAM & market-share dynamics** — wants share gains within a leading theme.
- **Product cycles as catalysts** — times around transitions (Hopper→Blackwell→Rubin); pricing power.
- **Forward-valuation sanity** — growth-adjusted, judged vs the opportunity set, not absolute P/E.
- **Management & accounting quality** — a hard filter (see kill switches).

## Kill switches (immediate exclude)

- **Accounting/governance red flags.** She trimmed/exited the "AI server maker" (Super Micro, +275%) over auditing issues, and exited a cybersecurity name before a breach. Accounting integrity is non-negotiable.
- These override momentum: a stock can be ripping and still be cut on a governance flag.

## Risk overlay (Ridley)

- **Concentration with conviction** — NVIDIA ran as high as ~20% at peak conviction, cut to ~5% for 2026 on opportunity-cost grounds.
- **Conviction through drawdowns** — held NVIDIA through a ~60% drawdown in 2022.
- **Two return streams** — equity-only (e.g., 56% in 2025) vs hedged/blended (37% audited in 2025). The hedge reduces upside in strong years but protects capital in drawdowns.
- **Automated hedge** since the 2022 selloff. Goal framed as "safely participate in tech."
- **Raising cash** as a second risk lever.
- **Technical timing** for entries/exits and systematic profit-taking.

## How she differs from a momentum/narrative trader

- Fundamentals-first with a *quantified forward model*; technicals are only a timing/risk overlay downstream of the thesis.
- Valuation discipline, growth-adjusted — will *reduce* a winner on valuation/opportunity-cost even when momentum is strong.
- Multi-year holding horizon; holds through deep drawdowns because the thesis, not the price trend, governs the hold.
- Hard fundamental kill switches override price strength.

## Distilled decision rules

For each candidate: (1) map the AI sub-trend and current bottleneck; (2) confirm leadership + moat; (3) build a forward revenue/earnings model; (4) derive upside via driver decomposition; (5) require attractive growth-adjusted forward valuation vs the opportunity set; (6) apply kill switches; (7) size by conviction (up to ~20%), trim on opportunity cost (down to ~5%); (8) overlay risk management (cash/hedge in deteriorating regimes); (9) hold through volatility while the thesis is intact, evolving the thesis as the bottleneck migrates.
