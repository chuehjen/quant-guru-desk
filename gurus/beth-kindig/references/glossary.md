# Glossary

## Fundamental / valuation terms

- **Forward revenue model** — pricing a stock off projected future revenue/earnings rather than trailing numbers. Kindig's core method.
- **Driver decomposition** — breaking implied upside into components (order gap × pricing × units → forward revenue → target). The "NVIDIA 70%" template.
- **Operating leverage** — revenue growing faster than operating costs, so margins expand with scale.
- **GAAP profitability breakthrough** — first time a company turns GAAP-profitable; a major re-rating catalyst.
- **Gross-margin trajectory** — direction of gross margin over time (expanding = key green flag).
- **TAM** — Total Addressable Market; the full revenue opportunity if a product captured all demand.
- **Growth-adjusted valuation** — judging a forward multiple relative to growth rate, not in absolute P/E terms.
- **Opportunity-cost test** — "is my capital better deployed elsewhere?" Drives trims of even structural winners.

## Moat / competitive terms

- **CUDA moat** — NVIDIA's software ecosystem lock-in; the archetype of a "winner-take-most" platform standard.
- **Platform standard** — the dominant interface/standard a market converges on (high switching costs).
- **Pure-play** — a company whose business *is* one trend (vs a diversified mega-cap with a side bet).

## Bottleneck / AI-buildout terms

- **Bottleneck migration** — capital rotating to the current binding constraint (compute → memory → power → software).
- **HBM** — High-Bandwidth Memory; stacked DRAM essential for AI accelerators.
- **Time to power** — how fast new electricity capacity can be brought online; Kindig's 2026 binding constraint.
- **Product cycle** — a hardware generation transition (Hopper→Blackwell→Rubin) that drives pricing power + unit growth.
- **Inference shift** — demand moving from model training to inference, changing the chip competitive landscape.

## Risk / portfolio terms

- **Risk overlay** — top-down hedging/cash management layered over bottom-up theses ("safely participate in tech").
- **Equity-only vs blended return** — un-hedged vs hedged portfolio return; the hedge reduces up-year upside but limits drawdowns.
- **Kill switch** — an accounting/governance red flag that forces an exit regardless of momentum.
- **Conviction weighting** — position size scales with thesis conviction (high-conviction names up to ~20%).
