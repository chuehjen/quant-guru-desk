# Bridgewater — Holdings & Track Record

## Bridgewater Associates overview

- Founded: 1975 by Ray Dalio
- AUM peak: ~$168B (2022); currently ~$120B (2025)
- Two main strategies: **Pure Alpha** (macro/tactical) and **All Weather** (risk parity)
- Ray Dalio transitioned from CIO → stepped back from day-to-day management (2022-2023)

## Strategy performance

### Pure Alpha (macro/tactical)

| Period | Annualized return (net) | Key characteristic |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| 1991-2024 | ~11.4% | Macro-directional, multi-asset, leveraged |
| 2008 | +9.5% (while S&P -37%) | Best year for relative performance |
| 2010-2019 | ~5-8% average | Underperformed equities in QE era |
| 2022 | +22% | Positioned for inflation + rate hikes |
| 2023 | ~3% | Cautious positioning, missed equity rally |

**Key insight**: Pure Alpha wins big during regime transitions (2008, 2022) but can lag during prolonged paradigms (2012-2019 QE era where "buy the dip" worked every time).

### All Weather (risk parity)

| Period | Annualized return (net) | Max drawdown | Key characteristic |
|--------|------------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1996-2024 | ~7.5% | ~12% (2022) | Much lower vol than equities |
| 2008 | ~-4% (vs S&P -37%) | | Diversification worked |
| 2022 | ~-14% (rates + stocks both crashed) | Worst year | Correlation breakdown |
| Long-term Sharpe | ~0.7 | | Better risk-adjusted than 60/40 |

**2022 lesson**: When BOTH stocks AND bonds fall simultaneously (rate hikes hitting everything), risk parity suffers because its core assumption (stocks/bonds negatively correlated) breaks. Gold was the only diversifier that worked.

## Bridgewater's 13F holdings (public equity portion, indicative)

Note: Bridgewater's 13F shows only the US equity/options portion, which is a small fraction of total AUM (most is in bonds, commodities, FX, and international markets).

### Typical characteristics of their equity book:

- **Heavily diversified** — often 500+ positions, no single stock > 2%
- **ETF-heavy** — SPY, IVV, EEM, VWO as broad market exposure
- **Sector tilts reflect macro view** — overweight/underweight sectors based on regime
- **Frequent turnover** — positions change quarterly as macro view evolves
- **Gold (GLD/IAU)** — consistently held as portfolio hedge

### Recent notable tilts (pattern, not specific holdings):

| Macro view | Equity positioning |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Inflation persists | Overweight: energy, materials, TIPS. Underweight: long-duration growth |
| Dollar weakening | Overweight: EM ETFs, international diversified. Underweight: domestic defensives |
| Late cycle | Reduce overall equity exposure, increase hedges, raise cash-like instruments |
| Goldilocks | Broad market exposure (SPY/QQQ), reduce gold, reduce commodities |

## Dalio's public statements (investment-relevant, 2024-2026)

### On US debt (consistent theme):
"The US is in the late stages of the big cycle. Debt levels are such that interest payments are becoming a larger and larger percentage of tax revenue. This doesn't end well — it ends with monetization and currency devaluation."

### On China:
"China is going through a classic deleveraging. The key question is whether it will be beautiful (managed) or ugly (deflationary). They have the tools — the question is political will."

### On the current paradigm:
"The paradigm of 'buy the dip because the Fed will save you' is broken. We're in a new paradigm where fiscal policy matters more than monetary policy, and inflation is a structural issue not easily solved."

### On AI:
"AI will increase productivity — that's the good news. The bad news is the wealth it creates will be extremely concentrated, worsening inequality, which is itself a late-cycle indicator."

### On portfolio construction:
"The holy grail of investing is finding 15+ uncorrelated return streams. If you can do that, you can dramatically improve your return-to-risk ratio."

## Famous calls (right and wrong)

### Correct:
- **2008 financial crisis** — warned about subprime, positioned defensively, profited
- **2022 inflation** — positioned for persistent inflation when consensus said "transitory"
- **European debt crisis (2011)** — identified sovereign debt unsustainability early
- **US-China great power competition** — flagged as structural shift years before consensus

### Incorrect or premature:
- **2019-2020 "paradigm shift" call** — warned cash is trash in Jan 2020; COVID crash followed immediately (though the money printing validated the longer-term thesis)
- **2013-2015 China hard landing** — worried about China debt crisis that was deferred by more stimulus
- **2023 "recession imminent"** — positioned cautiously while US economy remained resilient
- **Underperformance 2012-2019** — the QE paradigm persisted longer than anticipated

## Lessons for the agent

1. **Dalio is best at identifying the REGIME, not the exact timing** — regime calls may be 6-24 months early
2. **Risk parity works over decades but has bad years when correlations break** — 2022 was the worst
3. **"Cash is trash" vs "Cash is king" depends entirely on real rates** — there's no permanent view
4. **The macro overlay adds most value at TURNING POINTS** — in the middle of a paradigm, stock pickers outperform macro
5. **Diversification across environments > diversification within one environment** — owning 50 tech stocks is not diversification
