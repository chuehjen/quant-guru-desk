# Glossary — Dalio Macro-Systematic Investing

## Core framework concepts

**Economic machine (经济机器)**
Dalio's central metaphor: the economy is a machine driven by transactions. Every buyer's spending is a seller's income. Credit creates money temporarily (debt). The machine cycles through expansion and contraction because credit growth overshoots productivity growth, then corrects.

**Short-term debt cycle (短期债务周期)**
The 5-8 year business cycle driven by credit expansion and central bank policy. Expansion: easy credit → more spending → growth → inflation. Contraction: tight credit → less spending → recession → easing. The Fed manages this by raising/lowering rates.

**Long-term debt cycle (长期债务周期)**
The 75-100 year super-cycle where debt accumulates across multiple short-term cycles until the total burden becomes unsustainable. Each short-term cycle peak leaves behind more debt than the last. Culminates in a "big deleveraging" (last: 1929-1945; potentially building toward next).

**Deleveraging (去杠杆)**
The process of reducing debt relative to income when the debt burden becomes too heavy. Uses four levers: austerity, default, money printing, wealth transfers. Can be "beautiful" (balanced, growth maintained) or "ugly" (one lever dominates, extreme pain).

**Paradigm shift (范式转换)**
When the dominant market belief (paradigm) becomes unsustainable and everything changes. Markets adapt slowly, then suddenly. "Each paradigm lasts about 10 years, and the transition between them is extremely profitable for those who see it coming and extremely painful for those who don't."

**Risk parity (风险平价)**
An asset allocation approach that equalizes the RISK CONTRIBUTION of each asset class, not the dollar allocation. Since stocks are ~3x more volatile than bonds, a risk-parity portfolio holds more bonds (often with leverage) so that each asset contributes equally to portfolio volatility.

**All Weather (全天候策略)**
Bridgewater's flagship risk-parity strategy. Designed to perform reasonably well in all economic environments (growth up/down × inflation up/down) rather than betting on one scenario. Returns ~7.5% net with much lower drawdowns than equities alone.

## Macro indicators

**Yield curve (收益率曲线)**
The spread between long-term (10Y) and short-term (2Y) Treasury yields. Normal: positive (long > short, economy healthy). Flat: uncertainty. Inverted: short > long, recession signal (~70% predictive within 18 months). "The yield curve is the bond market's growth forecast."

**DXY (美元指数)**
Dollar index measuring USD against a basket of 6 major currencies (EUR 57.6%, JPY 13.6%, GBP 11.9%, CAD 9.1%, SEK 4.2%, CHF 3.6%). Strong dollar tightens global financial conditions because most global debt is dollar-denominated. Weak dollar eases conditions and helps EM/commodities.

**Credit spreads (信用利差)**
The yield difference between corporate bonds and Treasuries of equal maturity. Investment-grade (IG) spread: 80-150bps normal. High-yield (HY) spread: 300-500bps normal. Widening = stress rising, market pricing more defaults. >600bps HY = crisis territory.

**VIX (恐慌指数)**
The "fear index" — implied volatility of S&P 500 options over 30 days. <15 = complacency. 15-25 = normal. 25-35 = elevated fear. >35 = panic. "Low VIX is often the most dangerous time — complacency breeds vulnerability."

**M2 money supply (M2 货币供应)**
Broad measure of money in circulation (cash + checking + savings + money market). M2 growth rate matters more than level. Accelerating M2 → liquidity tailwind for assets. Shrinking M2 (QT) → headwind. "Inflation is always and everywhere a monetary phenomenon" (Friedman, which Dalio partially agrees with).

**ISM PMI (采购经理人指数)**
Leading indicator of manufacturing (ISM Mfg) and services (ISM Services) activity. >50 = expansion. <50 = contraction. <47 = recession territory. The NEW ORDERS sub-component leads the headline by 1-2 months.

**Fed Funds Rate (联邦基金利率)**
The overnight rate banks charge each other, set by the Fed. The most powerful short-term lever of the economic machine. Raising = tightening, slowing economy. Cutting = easing, stimulating economy. Zero-bound = QE becomes the tool. Current context matters vs history — 5.25% feels "restrictive" after a decade of ZIRP.

## Asset class concepts

**TIPS (通胀保值国债)**
Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities. Principal adjusts with CPI. Real yield (TIPS yield) = nominal yield minus inflation expectations. When real yields are negative → inflation expectations exceed nominal rates → good for gold. When real yields spike → headwind for all real assets.

**Gold as macro hedge (黄金的宏观对冲角色)**
Gold performs in three macro scenarios: (1) negative real rates (inflation > nominal rates), (2) currency debasement fears (money printing without growth), (3) systemic uncertainty (geopolitical or financial crisis). Gold UNDERPERFORMS in: positive real rate environments with strong growth (opportunity cost too high).

**Duration risk (久期风险)**
The sensitivity of a bond's price to interest rate changes. Long-duration bonds (TLT, 20Y+) move ~15-20% for a 1% rate change. Short-duration (SHV, 0-1Y) barely move. In a rising-rate environment, duration is the enemy. In a crisis with rate cuts, duration is the savior.

## Dalio-specific concepts

**Radical transparency (极度透明)**
Dalio's management principle applied to investing: acknowledge what you don't know, quantify your uncertainty, and never hide behind vague language. "I believe that the biggest problem that humanity faces is an ego sensitivity to finding out whether one is right or wrong."

**Believability-weighted decision making (可信度加权决策)**
Not all opinions are equal. Weight views by: (1) track record on similar decisions, (2) quality of reasoning articulated, (3) willingness to stress-test own view. In the context of this agent: weight macro indicators by their historical predictive power for the specific regime question.

**Pain + reflection = progress (痛苦+反思=进步)**
After every wrong call, diagnose WHY it was wrong: (1) Was the data misleading? (2) Was the framework wrong? (3) Was the timing off? (4) Was it unforeseeable? This applies to the reflection-memory system: track past regime calls and their outcomes.

**The big cycle (大周期)**
The 250-year cycle of reserve-currency empires: rise (strong education + innovation + competitiveness) → peak (financialization + inequality + debt) → decline (internal conflict + external challenge + currency debasement). Netherlands → Britain → US → ?. Not actionable on a daily timeframe but provides the backdrop for long-term asset allocation (e.g., gold allocation as reserve-currency-hedge).

**Cash is trash / Cash is king (现金是垃圾/现金为王)**
Dalio famously flip-flops on cash depending on the regime:
- **Cash is trash**: When real returns on cash are negative (inflation > money market rate). Hold assets instead.
- **Cash is king**: When nothing offers sufficient risk premium, or when a regime transition creates maximum uncertainty. Cash = optionality.
The correct call depends entirely on regime classification.
