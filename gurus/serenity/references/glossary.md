# Glossary

Plain-language definitions for recurring domain jargon. Define a term on first use in any deliverable.

## CPO / Optical Communication

- **CPO (Co-Packaged Optics)** — optical engine packaged directly with the switch/compute chip, reducing copper losses. "Using light to move data" inside AI servers.
- **Photonics** — devices/technologies that use light (not electricity) to transmit/process information.
- **Laser / Light source (ELS)** — the light-emitting component in CPO; often the supply bottleneck.
- **Optical interconnect** — chip-to-chip or rack-to-rack connections using light.
- **Transceiver / Pluggable** — converts electrical signals to optical; 1.6T refers to bandwidth class.
- **LRO (Linear Receive Optics)** — a class of optical module product line.
- **Optical engine / ELS** — functional module inside optical transceivers, often the target of M&A.
- **FAU (Fiber Array Unit)** — precisely aligns multiple optical fibers; a bottleneck when CPO ramps.
- **Packaging & test** — encapsulating and testing chips/optical devices; an overlooked bottleneck.
- **Substrate / Epiwafer** — upstream materials (SOI substrates, compound semiconductor epiwafers).

## Power Semiconductors

- **800VDC** — higher DC voltage in data centers to reduce power loss; critical as AI power demand surges.
- **Power semiconductor** — chips handling high voltage/current.
- **SiC / GaN (Silicon Carbide / Gallium Nitride)** — wide-bandgap materials ideal for high-voltage, high-efficiency power.
- **Foundry** — a factory that manufactures chips for others; capital-intensive.

## Valuation / Financials

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — the total market size a product/company could theoretically reach.
- **ASP (Average Selling Price)** — average price per unit; ASP growth = pricing-power signal.
- **Gross margin** — (revenue − COGS) / revenue; high margin often indicates pricing power.
- **P/B (Price-to-Book)** — stock price / book value per share; low P/B ≠ cheap (especially for foundries — look at ROIC).
- **ROIC (Return on Invested Capital)** — essential for capital-intensive companies.
- **Owner earnings** — Buffett's "true distributable cash flow" concept; the root of valuation.
- **ATM (At-The-Market offering)** — company issues new shares at market price; **dilutes** existing shareholders.
- **Dilution** — new shares diluting per-share value.
- **Ramp** — scaling production/revenue from small batches to mass production.

## Moat / Risk

- **Moat** — structural advantage that makes high returns sustainable and hard to compete away.
- **Switching cost** — the cost for a customer to change suppliers (certification, redesign, downtime risk).
- **Second source** — big customers usually cultivate backup suppliers, reducing single-supplier lock-in → raises "customer replacement risk".
- **Certification cycle / Qualification** — the process of a customer validating and designing-in a supplier; passing = higher switching cost.
- **Sole source / Primary source** — customer uses only/mainly one supplier; strong lock-in signal (still needs contract verification).

## Capital / Narrative (non-fundamental)

- **Index inclusion (MSCI / Nasdaq)** — passive funds must buy after inclusion; real catalyst but **non-fundamental**.
- **Uplisting / Dual-listing** — moving to another exchange for better liquidity.
- **Gamma / Short squeeze** — non-fundamental price surges from options hedging / short covering.
- **FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)** — negative narrative spreading panic; sentiment, not analysis.
- **OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)** — using public information (filings, transcripts, customer websites) to map supply-chain reality.

## Supply Chain & Infrastructure

- **CoWoS (Chip-on-Wafer-on-Substrate)** — TSMC's advanced packaging technology; the single biggest bottleneck in AI chip production.
- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory)** — stacked DRAM for AI accelerators; only 3 global suppliers (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron).
- **ABF substrate** — Ajinomoto Build-up Film substrate; critical material for advanced packaging (Unimicron, Ibiden).
- **EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography)** — ASML's monopoly technology for cutting-edge chip manufacturing.
- **InP (Indium Phosphide)** — compound semiconductor substrate material for optical devices; AXT Inc controls ~40-60% of global supply.
- **SiC (Silicon Carbide)** — wide-bandgap material for high-voltage power devices.
- **GaN (Gallium Nitride)** — wide-bandgap material for high-efficiency power conversion.

## Scoring & Evidence

- **Chokepoint Scorecard** — 8 positive factors + 8 penalty factors, quantitative scoring system (0-100 scale).
- **Evidence Ladder** — four-tier grading: Strong (filings/transcripts) → Medium (media/industry) → Weak (social/KOL) → Needs checking.
- **Red flag signals** — receivables/inventory growing faster than revenue, single unnamed customer rumors, stock primarily driven by social media.

## Controversy Terms

- **NINGI Research** — short-seller that published a report on $SIVE in June 2026 alleging artificial revenue.
- **Pump-and-dump** — buying a stock, promoting it to a large audience, then selling into the price surge. Allegation against Serenity-style social media stock promotion.
- **13F** — SEC filing required for institutional managers with >$100M AUM. Serenity is a retail investor, exempt from this disclosure.
- **Market impact** — the phenomenon where a large social media following can move small-cap stock prices just by posting about them.

## Classification labels (method-specific)

- **研究地图 (research map)** — a lead worth tracking; the default conclusion.
- **可投资结论 (investable conclusion)** — only after moat + financials + valuation + margin-of-safety work; **never** from posts alone.
- **unverified / 证据不足** — the default value for Buffett fields; no upgrade without cited evidence.
