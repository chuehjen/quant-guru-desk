# Worked Exemplars

Five annotated examples from the real corpus. Each shows the five-step method producing standard output. Use these as patterns — copy the **shape**, not the positions.

---

## 1. $SIVE: The textbook critical chokepoint

- **Step 1 (chokepoint).** Driver: AI compute → data movement → optical interconnect (CPO). Bottleneck: the laser/light source. SIVE is reportedly designed-in as sole/primary light-source supplier across JBL (1.6T LRO), Ayar Labs (after competitors removed from Ayar's site), GFS (listed as one of two laser suppliers), with Win Semi as the capacity partner. Market cap small (~$2B) vs the claimed opportunity → un-priced. All four chokepoint conditions plausibly hold.
- **Step 2 (first principles).** Strongest: supply bottleneck + designed into multiple supply chains. Weakest: realization — mostly 2027+ revenue, not current earnings.
- **Step 3 (Buffett gate).** Moat: medium, strengthening (certification + sole-source + capacity positioning). Profitability: `improving`, NOT proven (pipeline growth and margin targets are guidance, not cash flow). Customer replacement risk: medium-low. Buffett company: `not yet`. Conclusion: `研究地图`.
- **Step 4 (narrative hygiene).** Doubt ladder (customers → execution → market share → revenue → capacity) each rung falsified → re-rating; MSCI/Nasdaq inclusion = passive buying catalyst, non-fundamental. Neither lifts any Buffett field.
- **Step 5 (classify).** `研究地图`. Verify next: next-quarter revenue, capacity partner disclosure, customer contracts, dual-listing timeline, capex returns.

**Lesson:** The cleanest chokepoint — yet guidance/pipeline only earns `improving`, never `proven`. The method refuses to let a +300% chart promote the profitability field.

## 2. Foxconn → $Shunsin (6451): "Follow who actually does the work"

- **Step 1 (chokepoint).** Headline: Foxconn says CPO switches start Q3, ~10K units 2026. But Foxconn (~$123B) is too diluted. Follow the work: advanced optical packaging/test sits in subsidiary Shunsin (6451). That subsidiary is the un-priced expression.
- **Step 2 (first principles).** Strongest: if CPO ramps, packaging/test/FAU is the real bottleneck and value capture point, and the market only watches the brand = information gap. Weakest: Taiwan-listed liquidity, disclosure, currency, and geopolitical risk.
- **Step 3 (Buffett gate).** Moat: `unverified`. Profitability: `unverified`. Customer replacement: medium. Conclusion: `研究地图`.
- **Step 4 (narrative hygiene).** "Front-running the next supercycle" is a narrative framework, not delivery.

**Lesson:** The headline brand is the map, the subsidiary is the treasure. Always ask "who physically does the bottleneck work?"

## 3. $SOI (Soitec): Media FUD vs earnings

- **Step 1 (chokepoint).** Driver: silicon photonics substrates. Near-monopoly over SOI substrates; reorganizing for photonics-driven demand.
- **Step 3 (Buffett gate).** Moat: `unverified` → possibly medium (substrate monopoly needs evidence). Profitability: `unverified`.
- **Step 4 (narrative hygiene) — the point.** European media called it "overvalued/speculative/meme", then +342%. **Discipline: separate the "media being wrong" satisfaction from actual fundamentals.** The +342% re-rate does NOT promote any Buffett field.

**Lesson:** The most important discipline: a satisfying "I told you so" + a 4x chart is exactly when you must keep the fundamentals fields cold.

## 4. $XFAB: Government-stamped criticality + capital-intensity trap

- **Step 1 (chokepoint).** Driver: 800VDC → SiC/GaN. NIST/Dept-of-Commerce filings call XFAB the only high-volume SiC foundry in America. CHIPS Act 2 blueprints. ~$1.5B MC.
- **Step 2 (first principles).** Strongest: government-designated critical infrastructure + dual-continent subsidies. Weakest: capital intensity — foundry capex is heavy, don't judge on low P/B alone; need ROIC, utilization, margins; still in legacy (automotive) drag cycle.
- **Step 4 (narrative hygiene).** Government criticality (bullish, real) vs capital intensity (foundry not cheap just because P/B is low). Both must be surfaced.

**Lesson:** Two signals pull opposite ways. The method forces both into the open instead of cherry-picking the bullish one.

## 5. $AAOI: Real bottleneck, real dilution

- **Step 1 (chokepoint).** US-based photonics/laser capacity; laser bottleneck; possible long-term supply agreements with NVDA/AMD; fab-to-assembly all in America.
- **Step 2 (first principles).** Strongest: domestic vertical integration + laser bottleneck. Weakest: dilution — $600M ATM is real short-term pressure.
- **Step 4 (narrative hygiene) — the trap.** She gave one-year market cap "predictions" (e.g., $AAOI → $70B) and explicitly labeled them "random prediction". Method discipline: treat as scenario assumptions, NEVER write as price targets.

**Lesson:** Bottleneck value can be real while the headline price target is noise. When the source labels it a "random guess," the method keeps it labeled.

---

## Cross-cutting takeaways

1. Every exemplar lands on `研究地图`, never `可投资结论` — that is the method working.
2. The profitability field is most abused: pipeline/guidance/margin-targets are `improving` at most. Demand cited cash flow for `proven`.
3. The narrative-hygiene step separates this method from cheerleading.
