# Glossary

## Core ARK terms

- **Disruptive innovation** — "a technologically enabled new product or service that potentially changes the way the world works." ARK invests solely in this.
- **Innovation platform** — one of ARK's 5 mega-themes (AI, Robotics/Autonomous, Energy Storage, Public Blockchain, Multiomics/Genomics).
- **Convergence** — the thesis that platforms reinforce each other (AI as connective tissue); favor companies at platform intersections.
- **Pure-play** — a company that *is* the technology trend, not an incumbent with a side bet.
- **S-curve** — the adoption curve of a new technology: slow start → steep inflection → saturation.

## Wright's Law terms

- **Wright's Law** — cost falls by a constant % for every *cumulative doubling of units produced* (learning curve keyed to volume).
- **Learning rate** — the constant % cost decline per production doubling (e.g., ~20% for solar).
- **Moore's Law** — transistor density doubles ~every 2 years (time-indexed). ARK argues Wright's Law (volume-indexed) is more general.
- **Demand elasticity** — how much demand rises as price falls; the bridge from cost decline to volume explosion.

## Valuation / process terms

- **5-year horizon** — ARK's standard forecast window for every position.
- **15% CAGR hurdle** — minimum expected compound annual return over 5 years for a name to qualify/stay.
- **Expected value (EV) model** — bottom-up 5-year revenue/margin/cash-flow forecast → probability-weighted value (often Monte Carlo).
- **6-metric scoring** — People/Culture, Execution, Moat, Product Leadership, Thesis Risk, Valuation; aggregated to a cross-platform conviction rank.
- **Stock meeting** — ARK's open forum where analyst scores are debated and adjusted.

## Portfolio / behavior terms

- **Conviction weighting** — position size scales with composite score (top name ~10-12%, top 10 ≈ 50%+).
- **Buy the dip / buy weakness** — adding to a high-conviction name when it sells off but the thesis is intact (lower price → higher expected CAGR → higher score).
- **Trim winners** — reducing names that have run up (lower forward expected return) to recycle into higher-scored names.
- **Daily transparency / trade notifications** — ARK publishes full holdings daily and emails every trade.

## Competition / adaptation terms

- **Crypto-equity** — NYSE/NASDAQ-listed stocks that give blockchain exposure (COIN, HOOD, CRCL, XYZ, MSTR) — the allowed substitute for banned direct crypto.
- **ARKB** — ARK's spot-Bitcoin ETF; treated as crypto → BANNED in the competition.
- **TARK** — a leveraged ARK-adjacent product → BANNED (no leverage).
- **Dollar-weighted return** — the return the average investor actually earned (vs time-weighted fund return); the basis of the Morningstar critique.
