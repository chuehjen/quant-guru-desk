# Track Record, Criticism & Risk Cap

## The boom-bust signature

| Year | ARKK return | Context |
|------|------------|---------|
| 2020 | ~+150% | COVID-era growth/tech surge; most celebrated active manager of the year |
| 2021 | ~−23% | Rates rose; speculative growth de-rated |
| 2022 | ~−67% | Brutal drawdown (>70-80% peak-to-trough from early-2021 high) |
| 2023-2024 | Sharp rebounds | Recovery in up-tape years |
| 2025 | ~+35% | Again demonstrating extreme cyclicality |

ARKK behaves like a leveraged bet on long-duration growth and rates — extreme volatility, high beta.

## The most damning critique — the return gap

Morningstar's widely-cited analysis: ARKK posted a strong *time-weighted* return (~41% annualized over one 5-year window) but the *dollar-weighted* (actual investor) return was only **~9.9%** — because most investors bought *after* the 2020 run and suffered the drawdown. Morningstar titled the piece **"ARKK: An object lesson in how not to invest."** In the 2021 drawdown, shareholders suffered a ~12% dollar-weighted average loss, nearly 3× the fund's reported −4.3%.

**Lesson:** ARK's volatility and performance-chasing dynamics destroyed enormous shareholder value in dollar terms even when headline returns looked acceptable.

## Other standing criticisms

- **High beta / extreme volatility** — leveraged-like behavior to rates and risk sentiment.
- **Concentration & single-stock risk** — heavy Tesla dependence.
- **Liquidity concerns** in smaller holdings during outflows (ARK can become a forced seller and move thinly-traded names).
- **Forecast optimism** — TAM and price-target models are aggressive and rarely revised down enough; some early targets missed on the stated timeline.
- **Performance-chasing magnet** — the fund's marketing/transparency attracts hot money at tops.

## The risk cap (what the agent MUST add)

ARK's documented weakness is that **naive max-concentration + buy-every-dip is ruinous in a drawdown**. Reproduce the conviction, not the recklessness:

1. **Single-name cap ~10-12%** (don't let one name dominate beyond Tesla-flagship scale).
2. **Drawdown-aware cash buffer** — hold 5-15% cash when market breadth deteriorates or the tape is clearly risk-off, rather than being 100% invested into a falling market.
3. **Buy-the-dip with a budget** — add to thesis-intact losers, but cap how much fresh capital any single drawdown can absorb (avoid averaging down without limit).
4. **Respect the 15%/5-yr hurdle strictly** — if a re-rated name no longer clears it, trim rather than hold on narrative alone.

## How to use this in the agent

- Always disclose the boom-bust / dollar-weighted critique when discussing the strategy's track record — never present ARKK's headline time-weighted return as the investor experience.
- Keep ARK's *ranking and conviction logic*, but enforce the risk cap above so a competition portfolio survives drawdowns.
