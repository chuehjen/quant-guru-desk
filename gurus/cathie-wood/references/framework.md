# ARK / Cathie Wood — Analytical Framework

## Core philosophy: disruptive innovation

ARK defines disruptive innovation as "the introduction of a technologically enabled new product or service that potentially changes the way the world works." ARK invests **solely** in disruptive innovation — a single-strategy, thematic, actively-managed shop. The belief: innovation is **deflationary, exponential, and chronically mispriced** because traditional analysts anchor to linear/short-term models inside rigid sector silos.

## The five innovation platforms

1. **Artificial Intelligence** — neural networks, AI hardware/accelerators, agentic AI / AI-as-a-service
2. **Robotics & Autonomous Technology** — autonomous mobility (robotaxi), adaptive/humanoid robots, reusable rockets/space, 3D printing
3. **Energy Storage** — battery cost declines, EVs, autonomous logistics
4. **Public Blockchains** — Bitcoin/digital assets, smart contracts, wallets, DeFi
5. **Multiomic Sequencing / Genomics** — DNA sequencing, CRISPR/gene editing, gene & cell therapies, molecular diagnostics

These decompose into ~14-15 underlying technologies (tracked in the annual *Big Ideas* report).

## Convergence (the signature analytical move)

The platforms **reinforce each other** rather than evolving in isolation. AI is the connective tissue: it accelerates drug discovery (multiomics), trains self-driving systems (robotics + energy storage), and powers smart contracts (blockchain). Compounding converging S-curves → non-linear, exponential outcomes. **Favor companies at platform intersections** (Tesla = AI + robotics + energy storage; Tempus = AI + genomics).

## Wright's Law (the quantitative engine)

**Statement:** for every cumulative *doubling of units produced*, cost falls by a constant percentage (the "learning rate"). It is keyed to cumulative **production volume**, not calendar time.

- `Cost(next double) = Cost(now) × (1 − learning rate)`
- Solar PV ≈ 20% decline per doubling; batteries and DNA sequencing follow analogous curves.

**Why ARK prefers it over Moore's Law:** Moore's is time-indexed; Wright's is volume-indexed and (ARK argues) more general/accurate because cost reductions are *caused by* production/learning. "Moore's Law isn't dead — it's wrong; long live Wright's Law."

**The pipeline (cost curve → stock pick):**
1. Estimate the learning rate from historical cost data (EV batteries, AI training/inference compute, DNA sequencing, robots).
2. Project the cost-decline curve forward as production scales.
3. Translate falling cost → demand elasticity → adoption S-curve (cost crosses affordability threshold → demand inflects → volumes explode → recursive further cost decline = virtuous cycle).
4. Size the TAM at the new price point; estimate which companies capture share/margin.
5. Build a 5-year revenue/margin/cash-flow model → expected value.

**Worked example — Tesla:** ~85% learning curve (15% cost decline per doubling) to forecast auto gross margins, then Monte Carlo across business lines. AI inference cost falling on the order of ~69%/yr in some ARK analyses underpins AI adoption forecasts.

## The valuation hurdle

A security must clear a **minimum ~15% compound annual return over a 5-year horizon** to qualify and stay in the portfolio. The 5-year horizon and 15% CAGR floor are explicit, hard, reproducible gates. Build forecasts bottom-up to a 5-year price target, then compare to this hurdle.

## Qualification gates (top-down, before scoring)

A company must be: exposed to genuinely *disruptive* tech (not incremental); positioned for *exponential* growth; a *leader/credible disruptor* within one of the five platforms; in a *large, rapidly expanding TAM* with a long runway; expected to deliver 3-5 years of growth and clear the ~15% CAGR hurdle.

## Process notes

- **Iterative & open.** Analyst scores are debated in open "stock meetings," continuously re-validated as new cost data / milestones / fundamentals arrive — rankings and weights change frequently.
- **Cross-platform ranking.** Every security is ranked against every other across all platforms (apples-to-apples conviction ranking).
- **Radical daily transparency.** ARK publishes full holdings every day and emails trade notifications (every buy/sell, by share count). This makes ARK unusually easy to mirror — use it.
