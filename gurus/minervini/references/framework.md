# Minervini SEPA Framework

## The SEPA philosophy

**Specific Entry Point Analysis** — the idea that WHEN you buy matters as much as WHAT you buy. A great stock bought at the wrong time (extended, late-stage, wrong market environment) can lose you 30% before you're proven right. A mediocre stock bought at the perfect entry (VCP pivot, Stage 2 confirmation, volume surge) can make you 50% quickly with minimal drawdown.

"I want the lowest-risk entry with the highest probability of immediate follow-through."

## The complete SEPA decision flow

```
1. Market environment → BEARISH? → Go to cash (80-100%)
                      → BULLISH? ↓
2. Stage Analysis → NOT Stage 2? → Pass. No exceptions.
                  → Stage 2 confirmed? ↓
3. Trend Template → ANY criterion fails? → Watchlist, not ready.
                  → All 8 pass? ↓
4. Fundamental filter → Earnings/revenue weak? → Pass (technicals without fundamentals = empty)
                      → Fundamentals confirm? ↓
5. VCP forming? → NO VCP visible → Watchlist. Wait for contraction pattern.
               → VCP developing but not ready → Set price alert at pivot.
               → VCP complete, pivot identifiable ↓
6. Entry plan → Set buy order at pivot price
             → Set stop-loss (below last contraction low)
             → Calculate position size (risk budget method)
             → Set target (+20-25% for partial, trail remainder)
             → EXECUTE when pivot breaks on volume
```

## Market environment assessment

Before ANY individual stock analysis, assess the general market:

### Bull market criteria (Minervini's market model)
- S&P 500 above rising 50-day and 200-day MA
- New 52-week highs > new 52-week lows (on NYSE)
- Advance-decline line trending up
- Leading stocks (SEPA qualifiers) making new highs
- Market breadth expanding (% stocks above 200MA > 60%)

### When to reduce exposure
| Signal | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Distribution days cluster (4+ in 2 weeks) | Reduce to 50% exposure |
| S&P below 50-day MA + MA flattening | Reduce to 30% exposure |
| More new lows than new highs for 5+ days | Reduce to 20% or less |
| S&P below declining 200-day MA | 0-10% exposure (cash is a position) |

"The market doesn't owe you an opportunity. Sometimes the best trade is no trade."

## Stage Analysis — detailed identification

### Stage 1 → Stage 2 transition signals
The most profitable moment — catching the start of a new uptrend:
1. Price breaks above flat/declining 200-day MA on HEAVY volume (2x+ average)
2. 50-day MA crosses above 200-day MA ("golden cross" — but only meaningful WITH other signals)
3. Earnings surprise or catalyst triggers the move
4. Stock makes a new 52-week high for the first time in > 6 months
5. Relative strength line begins trending up (outperforming SPX)

### Stage 2 — the only stage to own
Characteristics of a healthy Stage 2:
- **Staircase pattern**: higher highs AND higher lows on the weekly chart
- **MA support**: pullbacks find support at 10-week or 20-week MA
- **Volume pattern**: heavy on advances, light on pullbacks (accumulation)
- **Earnings**: growing, ideally accelerating
- **Relative strength**: consistently outperforming the market

### Stage 2 → Stage 3 warning signs (sell signals)
1. Climax top: parabolic advance on record volume followed by reversal
2. 50-day MA flattens and price starts closing below it repeatedly
3. Wide, volatile swings (high-range bars both directions)
4. Volume increases on DOWN days
5. Stock makes a new high but relative strength makes a LOWER high (bearish divergence)
6. "Churning": high volume with no price progress (distribution at the top)

### Stage 3 → Stage 4 confirmation (get out immediately)
- Price decisively below 200-day MA
- 50-day MA crosses below 200-day MA
- Lower highs AND lower lows on weekly
- Rallies fail at declining MAs
- Fundamentals deteriorating (earnings misses, guidance cuts)

## VCP — advanced pattern mechanics

### The psychology behind VCP
Each contraction represents a battle between buyers and sellers:
- **1st contraction (deepest)**: Initial selling wave. Weak holders exit. Heavy volume.
- **2nd contraction (shallower)**: Remaining sellers try again but fewer shares available. Less volume.
- **3rd contraction (tightest)**: Sellers exhausted. Almost no volume. Supply absorbed.
- **Breakout**: Buyers overwhelm the tiny remaining supply. Volume explodes. Price gaps up.

"The VCP works because it identifies the exact moment when supply is exhausted and demand is about to overwhelm."

### VCP measurement rules

| Metric | Ideal | Acceptable | Disqualifying |
|--------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| # of contractions | 3-4 | 2 or 5 | 1 (not a VCP) or 6+ (too many failures) |
| 1st contraction depth | 15-25% | 10-35% | > 50% (too damaged) |
| Final contraction depth | 5-10% | 3-15% | > 20% (not contracting) |
| Volume in final tight area | 40-60% below average | Down 25-70% | ABOVE average (distribution) |
| Base duration | 5-15 weeks | 3-26 weeks | < 2 weeks (too short) or > 52 weeks (dead money) |
| Price vs MAs in base | Above 150/200 MA | Above 200 MA | Below 200 MA (not Stage 2) |

### The pivot point — where to buy

The pivot = the highest price in the final tight contraction area. NOT the all-time high of the base.

Rules for valid pivot breakout:
1. Price closes above pivot
2. Volume on breakout day ≥ 50% above 50-day average (ideally 100%+)
3. Price action is decisive (close in upper 25% of day's range)
4. Breakout NOT on a gap that's too far from pivot (> 5% above = chase territory)

### Power plays (even better than standard VCP)

A "power play" is when a stock barely pulls back (< 5-7% from high) before breaking out again:
- Extremely tight consolidation (3-7 trading days)
- Volume completely dries up
- Stock is already in an accelerating Stage 2 advance
- Break to new high on volume → buy immediately
- Tightest stop possible (below consolidation low, often just 3-5%)

## Risk management — the survival system

### Position sizing by risk budget

The #1 rule: **Never risk more than 1-2% of total portfolio on a single trade.**

```
Step 1: Define risk tolerance → 1.5% of $10,000 = $150
Step 2: Identify stop distance → Entry $80, Stop $74 = $6 risk per share (7.5%)
Step 3: Calculate shares → $150 / $6 = 25 shares
Step 4: Position size → 25 × $80 = $2,000 (20% of portfolio)
```

This means:
- Tight stop (5%) → larger position (can be 25-30% of portfolio)
- Wide stop (10%) → smaller position (15% of portfolio)
- Very wide stop (15%) → tiny position (10%) or SKIP (setup not tight enough)

### Stop-loss philosophy

"The stop-loss is your rent for being in the market. Pay it without emotion."

| Stop type | Placement | When to use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Initial hard stop | Below last contraction low (or pivot minus 7-8%) | On entry — ALWAYS |
| Trailing stop (after +10%) | Below 10-day or 20-day MA | Protect partial gains |
| Trailing stop (after +25%) | Below 50-day MA | Let winner run |
| Profit target (partial) | +20-25% from entry | Sell 50%, trail rest |
| Climax sell | Immediate full exit | Parabolic move + exhaustion volume |

### Loss limits

| Rule | Trigger | Action |
|------|---------|--------|
| Single trade max loss | -7 to -8% from entry | Sell all. No exceptions. |
| Weekly portfolio loss limit | -5% of total portfolio in one week | Reduce all positions by 50%. Go defensive. |
| Monthly portfolio loss limit | -10% of total portfolio in one month | Go 100% cash. Re-evaluate after 2 weeks minimum. |

## Pyramiding — adding to winners

The ONLY time to add to a position:
1. Stock breaks out of ANOTHER VCP after the initial entry (base-on-base)
2. You are already profitable on the first entry (never add to a loser)
3. Stop-loss on new shares is above your original entry (locked in profit)
4. Total position still within 25% single-stock limit

"I want to be biggest in my best positions and smallest in my worst."

## When Minervini does NOTHING

- Market in correction → 80-100% cash. "Cash is a position."
- No VCP setups qualifying → 100% cash. "The gun is loaded but there's nothing to shoot."
- 3 consecutive stopped-out trades → step back, reduce size by 50%, reassess
- Earnings season uncertainty → don't hold through earnings unless willing to gap-down through stop
