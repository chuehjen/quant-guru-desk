# SEPA Scorecard — Minervini Agent

A quantitative scoring system for ranking trade candidates through Minervini's momentum/timing lens. Unlike fundamental scorecards, this evaluates SETUP QUALITY and TIMING READINESS, not intrinsic value.

## Positive factors (8 factors, 0-5 each, weighted to 100)

### 1. Stage 2 confirmation (weight: 16%)

Is the stock in a confirmed, healthy Stage 2 uptrend?

- 5: All 8 Trend Template criteria pass cleanly; staircase pattern of higher highs/lows visible on weekly; MAs perfectly stacked (50 > 150 > 200, all rising)
- 4: 7/8 criteria pass, one borderline; overall trend clearly up
- 3: Stage 2 but young (recently transitioned from Stage 1); MAs still converging
- 2: Mixed signals — price above MAs but MAs not all rising yet; possible early Stage 2
- 1: Stage 1 (basing) or Stage 3 (topping) — not ready
- 0: Stage 4 (declining) — DO NOT TOUCH

### 2. VCP quality (weight: 18%)

How textbook is the volatility contraction pattern?

- 5: 3-4 clearly defined contractions, each measurably tighter than prior; volume dried up 50%+ in final tight area; pivot clearly identifiable; duration 5-15 weeks
- 4: Good VCP with 2-3 contractions tightening; volume declining; minor imperfections but tradeable
- 3: Developing VCP — 1-2 contractions visible, might need 1-2 more weeks to complete
- 2: Loose consolidation — some contraction but not clean; wide swings still present
- 1: No recognizable VCP — just a pullback or a choppy range
- 0: Pattern broken — contraction WIDENED (sellers returning), or price below 200-day MA during "base"

### 3. Earnings acceleration (weight: 14%)

Is EPS growth rate ACCELERATING (not just growing)?

- 5: Latest quarter EPS up 50%+ YoY AND accelerating from prior quarter's growth rate; next quarter estimates up too
- 4: EPS up 30-50% YoY and accelerating; positive analyst estimate revisions
- 3: EPS up 20-30% YoY; growth stable but not clearly accelerating
- 2: EPS up 10-20% YoY; or accelerating from a low base (turnaround)
- 1: EPS flat or up < 10%; or decelerating from a high rate
- 0: EPS negative or declining; no earnings catalyst visible

### 4. Relative strength (weight: 12%)

Is this stock outperforming the market?

- 5: RS rating 90+ (top 10% of all stocks vs SPX over 6 months); price at/near 52-week high while market flat
- 4: RS rating 80-89; clearly outperforming sector and market
- 3: RS rating 70-79; in-line with strong sector but not standing out individually
- 2: RS rating 50-69; underperforming the market; not a leader
- 1: RS rating < 50; lagging badly; institutional exodus
- 0: Making new lows while market makes new highs; absolute weakness

### 5. Volume signature (weight: 12%)

Does the volume pattern show accumulation (smart money buying)?

- 5: Up-days on 150%+ average volume; down-days on 50% average volume; textbook accumulation
- 4: Clear accumulation pattern — heavier volume on advances, lighter on declines
- 3: Volume neutral — no clear accumulation or distribution signal
- 2: Mixed — some distribution days (heavy volume on down moves) interspersed
- 1: Distribution pattern — heavier volume on declines than advances
- 0: Obvious distribution — multiple high-volume sell-offs, institutional dumping

### 6. Price proximity to pivot (weight: 10%)

How close to the ideal buy point?

- 5: Within 2% of pivot AND volume starting to contract (ready to break any day)
- 4: Within 5% of pivot; setup is live, actionable this week
- 3: Within 10% of pivot; forming but not quite ready (1-2 weeks out)
- 2: 10-15% from pivot; VCP still developing, need patience
- 1: > 15% below pivot OR > 5% above pivot (already broke out — missed it or chasing)
- 0: No identifiable pivot point; stock in freefall or random chop

### 7. Revenue growth (weight: 9%)

Top-line growth confirms fundamental strength.

- 5: Revenue up 30%+ YoY and accelerating; subscription/recurring revenue growing
- 4: Revenue up 20-30% YoY; sustainable drivers identified
- 3: Revenue up 10-20% YoY; steady but not explosive
- 2: Revenue up 5-10% YoY; or lumpy/contract-dependent
- 1: Revenue flat or declining < 5%
- 0: Revenue declining; secular headwinds; market shrinking

### 8. Institutional sponsorship (weight: 9%)

Are big funds buying?

- 5: Increasing fund ownership 3 consecutive quarters + recent analyst upgrades + hedge fund 13F additions
- 4: Rising institutional ownership + at least 1 recent upgrade or notable fund addition
- 3: Stable institutional ownership; no major changes
- 2: Flat or slightly declining institutional ownership
- 1: Institutional ownership dropping; downgrades; funds exiting
- 0: No institutional ownership (micro-cap orphan) or mass exodus

## Penalty factors (6 penalties, 0-5 each, multiplied by 2.5x weight)

### 1. Late-stage base (4th+ base)
- 5: 5th+ base since the Stage 2 advance began — extremely high failure rate (>70%)
- 3: 4th base — elevated failure rate (~50%). Requires perfect VCP + explosive volume to overcome.
- 1: 3rd base — still acceptable if VCP is tight. Standard success rate.
- 0: 1st or 2nd base — ideal. Highest success rate for breakouts.

### 2. Overhead supply
- 5: Massive resistance (prior high formed with heavy volume 6-12 months ago, many trapped buyers)
- 3: Moderate resistance (prior high exists but volume was average; some supply to absorb)
- 1: Light resistance (stock near all-time highs; minimal trapped sellers)
- 0: No overhead supply — stock at all-time highs; every holder is profitable

### 3. Excessive extension from moving averages
- 5: Price > 50% above 50-day MA — extremely overextended, crash risk
- 3: Price 25-50% above 50-day MA — extended; pullback likely before next buyable setup
- 1: Price 10-25% above 50-day MA — modestly extended but within normal range for leaders
- 0: Price within 10% of 50-day MA — ideal buying range after VCP contraction

### 4. Wide/loose pattern
- 5: "VCP" contractions NOT getting tighter; last contraction is WIDER than prior — pattern failure
- 3: Contractions exist but inconsistent; one tightened then loosened; sloppy but salvageable
- 1: Slightly loose in one contraction but overall trend toward tightening
- 0: Perfect VCP tightening — each contraction measurably shallower than prior

### 5. Market headwind
- 5: General market in Stage 4 decline; buying individual stocks is swimming upstream
- 3: Market in correction (below 50-day, breadth deteriorating) but not in full bear
- 1: Market healthy but sector-specific headwind (rotation out of this group)
- 0: Market in strong uptrend, broad participation, leaders breaking out

### 6. Earnings deceleration
- 5: EPS growth rate halved or worse vs prior quarter (e.g., +80% → +30%); "post-peak" earnings
- 3: EPS growth rate slowed moderately (e.g., +50% → +35%); still growing but deceleration visible
- 1: EPS growth rate flat (same as prior quarter); not accelerating but not decelerating
- 0: EPS growth rate accelerating — each quarter's YoY growth exceeds the prior quarter's

## Rating scale

| Score | Verdict | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 85-100 | A+ Setup | Buy at pivot with full position size; textbook trade |
| 70-84 | Solid setup | Buy with standard size; slightly wider parameters acceptable |
| 55-69 | Developing | Watchlist only; set price alert at pivot; may become actionable in 1-2 weeks |
| 40-54 | Not ready | Pass. Wait for tighter pattern or better market environment |
| < 40 | Avoid | Do not trade; either broken Stage 2 or terrible market conditions |
