# Glossary — Minervini SEPA Momentum

## Core concepts

**SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis / 精确入场点分析)**
Minervini's complete methodology for identifying the optimal moment to buy a stock. Not just "buy good stocks" but "buy good stocks at the exact right moment with minimal risk." The combination of Stage Analysis (trend) + VCP (pattern) + risk management (survival).

**Stage Analysis (阶段分析)**
Classifying every stock into one of four life-cycle stages: Stage 1 (basing), Stage 2 (advancing), Stage 3 (topping), Stage 4 (declining). Only Stage 2 is ownable. Originally from Stan Weinstein's work, refined by Minervini with more granular criteria.

**VCP — Volatility Contraction Pattern (波动收缩形态)**
The specific price/volume pattern that signals sellers are exhausted and a breakout is imminent. Each "contraction" (pullback) within a base gets shallower and quieter until supply is completely absorbed. The name describes the physics: volatility literally contracts before it explodes.

**Pivot point (枢纽价/买入支点)**
The precise price level where a VCP breakout is confirmed. It's the high of the final tight contraction area — the "line in the sand" where supply is definitively overcome. Buy above this price on volume. Not to be confused with the 52-week high (which may be much higher if the base is deep).

**Trend Template (趋势模板)**
Minervini's 8-point checklist that mechanically confirms a stock is in Stage 2. All 8 must pass. No judgment, no exceptions. This removes subjectivity from trend identification.

## Technical terms

**Moving averages (移动平均线)**
- **50-day MA (50日均线)**: Short-term trend. Price above = short-term bullish. Pullbacks to 50-day in Stage 2 are often buying opportunities.
- **150-day MA (150日均线)**: Intermediate trend. Equivalent to 30-week MA on weekly chart. The "Weinstein line."
- **200-day MA (200日均线)**: Long-term trend. The dividing line between bull and bear for a stock. Above = Stage 2 possible. Below = Stage 4 likely.
- **MA stacking (均线多头排列)**: 50 > 150 > 200, all rising. The signature of a healthy Stage 2 advance.

**Relative Strength (相对强度, RS)**
A stock's price performance relative to the S&P 500 (or market index) over a defined period (typically 6 months or 1 year). RS 90 = outperforming 90% of all stocks. Minervini demands RS > 70, prefers > 80. Leaders lead.

**Volume dry-up (成交量萎缩)**
When volume drops significantly (40-60% below average) during a pullback or tight area. Indicates sellers are exhausted — no one left to sell. Precursor to a breakout. "Quiet before the storm."

**Breakout (突破)**
Price moving above the pivot point on above-average volume (50%+ above 50-day average). Confirms the VCP pattern is complete and the next leg up has begun. Must be decisive — weak volume breakouts often fail.

**Distribution day (分派日)**
A day where the index/stock falls on volume higher than the prior day. Indicates institutional selling. 4-5 distribution days within 2-3 weeks = market correction likely starting.

**Accumulation day (吸筹日)**
A day where the index/stock rises on volume higher than the prior day. Indicates institutional buying. Clusters of accumulation days = healthy demand.

**Churning (高位洗盘)**
High volume with no price progress. Price closes near where it opened despite heavy trading. Indicates distribution at a top — institutions selling into retail demand. Major warning sign in Stage 3.

## Risk management terms

**Risk budget (风险预算)**
The maximum dollar amount you're willing to lose on a single trade. Expressed as a percentage of total portfolio (1-2%). This number determines position size via the formula: shares = risk budget / (entry price - stop price).

**R-multiple (R 倍数)**
The ratio of reward to risk on a trade. If you risk $4/share (stop distance) and gain $12/share, that's a 3R trade. Minervini targets minimum 3:1 R:R on initial entries.

**Stop-loss (止损)**
The predetermined price at which you exit a losing trade. Set BEFORE entry. Never moved down, only up. "The stop is not where you think you'll be wrong — it's where the pattern is PROVEN wrong."

**Trailing stop (移动止损)**
A stop that moves UP as the stock advances, locking in profits. Typically follows 10-day MA (aggressive), 20-day MA (moderate), or 50-day MA (wide). Gives winners room to run while protecting against reversals.

**Position sizing (仓位管理)**
Determining how many shares to buy based on: (1) portfolio risk tolerance (1-2%), (2) stop distance, and (3) maximum single-stock allocation (25%). Tight stops → bigger positions. Wide stops → smaller positions. This is how you stay in the game.

**Pyramiding (金字塔加仓)**
Adding to a winning position as it proves itself. Only add when: (a) already profitable, (b) stock breaks out of a new VCP (base-on-base), (c) new stop is above original entry. Never add to a loser.

## Pattern terms

**Base (底部整理)**
A period of sideways consolidation after an advance. The stock "digests" its gains while weak holders exit and strong hands accumulate. A base that resolves UPWARD is bullish. Multiple bases within a Stage 2 advance is normal (1st, 2nd, 3rd base).

**Base-on-base (底部叠底)**
A VCP that forms DIRECTLY on top of a prior breakout without giving back much ground. Shows extreme demand — the stock barely pulled back before setting up again. Often the most powerful patterns.

**Cup-with-handle (杯柄形态)**
A rounded base (the "cup") followed by a short, shallow pullback (the "handle"). A specific type of VCP where the cup represents the major contraction and the handle is the final tight area before breakout. William O'Neil's classic pattern.

**Power play (强势整理突破)**
An extremely tight (3-7 day) consolidation near the highs of a move, followed by breakout on volume. The stock is so strong it barely pauses. Requires very tight stops (3-5%) and very fast execution. "When a stock won't give you a pullback, that IS the pullback."

**Climax top (顶部放量冲顶)**
A parabolic advance (price accelerating upward at unsustainable pace) on record volume, followed by a reversal. This is the SELL signal for momentum traders. Often the largest single-day volume in the stock's history. "The exhaustion of the last buyer."

## Market concepts

**Follow-through day (跟进日)**
After a market correction, the first day where the index rallies 1.5%+ on volume higher than the prior day (typically day 4-7 of an attempted rally). Confirms the correction is likely over and a new uptrend is starting. Not foolproof (30% fail) but necessary signal.

**Market correction (市场回调)**
A decline of 10-20% in the major indexes from recent highs. Minervini's approach during corrections: raise cash aggressively (80-100%), protect profits, wait for follow-through day before re-deploying. "Surviving a correction with your capital intact IS alpha."

**Sector rotation (板块轮动)**
The tendency of market leadership to shift between sectors as economic conditions change. Minervini follows WHERE the breakouts are happening — if tech is leading, that's where setups will be. If energy is leading, rotate there. No loyalty to sectors.
