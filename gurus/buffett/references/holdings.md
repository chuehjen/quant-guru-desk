## Berkshire's cash position philosophy

Buffett maintains enormous cash reserves ($300B+ in 2025) when:
- Market valuations are high (S&P Shiller PE > 30)
- No wonderful businesses are available at fair prices
- Optionality value of cash during future crises > expected return of mediocre investments

"We never want to count on the kindness of strangers. Cash is a call option with no expiration date."

## Key financial metrics (Berkshire as benchmark)

- Berkshire's book value CAGR (1965-2024): ~19.8%
- S&P 500 total return CAGR (1965-2024): ~10.2%
- Berkshire outperformance: ~2x S&P over 59 years
- Recent underperformance (2010-2020) vs S&P: concentrated tech rally that Buffett mostly missed
- 2022-2025 outperformance: value rotation + energy + insurance cycle + massive cash deployment opportunity
