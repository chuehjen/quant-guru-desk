# Glossary — Buffett Value Investing

## Core concepts

**Owner earnings (所有者盈余)**
Buffett's preferred measure of true economic earnings. Net income + depreciation/amortization − maintenance capex − working capital increases. Represents what an owner could extract without degrading the business. More honest than EBITDA, which "ignores the tooth fairy question of who pays for capital expenditures."

**Intrinsic value (内在价值)**
The discounted present value of all future owner earnings a business will generate from now until judgment day. Not a precise number — a range. "Two people looking at the same set of facts will almost inevitably come up with at least slightly different intrinsic value figures."

**Margin of safety (安全边际)**
The gap between market price and intrinsic value. The concept (from Graham) that protects against errors in judgment, bad luck, and unforeseen events. Buy at enough discount that even if you're wrong about growth, you still don't lose money. Minimum 20-30% for quality businesses; 30-40% for mediocre ones.

**Circle of competence (能力圈)**
The boundary of what you can truly understand well enough to value. Not about intelligence — about self-awareness. "What counts is not how much you know, but how honestly you define what you don't know." Outside the circle = no opinion, no investment, no exceptions.

**Moat (护城河)**
A structural competitive advantage that protects a business's pricing power and market position from competitors. Must be durable (not just current) and wide (not easily crossed). Four types: brand/pricing power, switching costs, network effects, cost advantage.

**Mr. Market (市场先生)**
Graham's allegory: the stock market is a manic-depressive business partner who offers to buy or sell his share every day at a different price. You can take advantage of his moods or ignore him — but never let him guide your judgment of value. "The market is there to serve you, not to instruct you."

## Financial metrics

**ROE (净资产收益率)**
Return on equity. Net income / shareholders' equity. Buffett demands >15% sustained over 10 years WITH low leverage. High ROE from high debt is accounting illusion, not business quality.

**ROIC (投入资本回报率)**
Return on invested capital. More rigorous than ROE because it includes debt capital. ROIC > WACC = value creation. ROIC < WACC = value destruction. The moat shows up as ROIC persistently above peers and above cost of capital.

**Debt/equity ratio (债务权益比)**
Total long-term debt / shareholders' equity. Buffett prefers < 0.5 for operating companies. "In the insurance business, you can get into a lot of trouble using leverage. In any business, frankly."

**Interest coverage (利息覆盖倍数)**
EBIT / interest expense. Must be > 5x in normal times. If a recession could push it below 3x → the company is not a fortress.

**Maintenance capex (维护性资本支出)**
The capital expenditure required merely to maintain current earning power — NOT to grow. Often hidden within total capex. Estimate by: (a) management guidance, (b) depreciation as a proxy, (c) industry benchmarks. Growth capex is separate and optional.

## Behavioral concepts

**Temperament over IQ (气质重于智商)**
"Investing is not a game where the guy with the 160 IQ beats the guy with the 130 IQ. Once you have ordinary intelligence, what you need is the temperament to control the urges that get other people into trouble."

**Fear and greed cycle (恐惧与贪婪周期)**
"Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful." In practice: build cash when markets are euphoric (Buffett's Berkshire cash peaked at $325B in 2024); deploy aggressively during panics (bought Goldman Sachs and GE at crisis terms in 2008).

**The 20-slot punch card (20次打卡规则)**
"I could improve your ultimate financial welfare by giving you a ticket with only 20 slots in it so that you had 20 punches — representing all the investments that you got to make in a lifetime." Forces concentration and extreme selectivity.

**Sit on your hands (坐着不动)**
"The stock market is designed to transfer money from the active to the patient." Inactivity is not laziness — it's discipline. Buffett made most of his wealth from fewer than 20 decisions over 60 years.

## Valuation terms

**DCF (现金流折现)**
Discounted cash flow. The theoretically correct way to value any asset: sum of all future cash flows discounted to present value. Buffett uses 10% discount rate (his opportunity cost) and conservative growth assumptions. "All intelligent investing is value investing — acquiring more than you are paying for."

**Terminal value (终值)**
The value of all cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period (typically year 10+). Use Gordon Growth Model: terminal CF × (1 + g) / (r - g). g should never exceed 4% for terminal (long-term GDP + inflation). Terminal value often > 60% of total intrinsic value — hence the importance of getting the terminal growth rate right.

**Earnings yield (盈利收益率)**
Inverse of P/E ratio. If a stock trades at P/E 20, its earnings yield is 5%. Compare to the 10-year Treasury yield. If the earnings yield < Treasury yield AND the business isn't growing fast → the stock is expensive. Buffett has used this as a rough "is the market cheap?" gauge for decades.

**Book value per share (每股账面价值)**
Total shareholders' equity / shares outstanding. Historically Buffett's favorite metric for Berkshire (used in annual letter table from 1965-2018). Less relevant for asset-light businesses, but still a floor for asset-heavy industrials and financials.
